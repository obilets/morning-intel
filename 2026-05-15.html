The Agent Pricing War: Anthropic Meters, OpenAI Undercuts

Anthropic announced that starting June 15, all programmatic Claude usage — via Agent SDK, headless mode, GitHub Actions, or third-party frameworks — will be billed against a separate monthly credit pool at API-style rates. Pro subscribers get $20 in credits, Max 5x users $100, and Max 20x users $200. Unused credits expire monthly. If the pool runs dry, overage tokens bill at full API rates.

OpenAI moved fast to exploit the opening. Its counter-announcement emphasizes standard API pricing with no separate runtime fees and no session-hour meter, with the SDK remaining free and open source. Meanwhile, OpenAI's Pro $100 tier — priced to match Claude Max 5x — includes a temporary 10x Codex promotion through May 31. The message is clear: as AI agents consume compute far faster than humans ever could, the era of unlimited AI subscriptions may be ending.

→ Impact: This pricing split matters for every product built on top of AI models. If agentic usage gets metered separately, the cost calculus for AI-powered email triage, calendar management, and meeting intelligence changes fundamentally. Our product's AI features need a pricing architecture that scales sustainably as agents run continuously — not just when users are actively engaged.

Goldman Sachs: AI's $750B Buildout Hits a Human Wall

The most expensive technology buildout in history is running into a problem Silicon Valley can't code its way out of: there aren't enough humans to build the physical infrastructure AI needs. Goldman Sachs estimates 760,000 additional power and grid workers are needed by 2030, including 207,000 specialized transmission and distribution roles requiring three to four years of training. That pipeline doesn't exist — and can't be built quickly.

The numbers are sobering. Nearly half of all U.S. data centers planned for 2026 have been canceled or delayed. Of the 12 GW of AI data center capacity announced this year, only about 5 GW is under active construction. Lead times for high-voltage transformers have stretched from 12–18 months to 36–48 months. AI-related capex is expected to surpass $750 billion in 2026, but the physical world is becoming the binding constraint on AI's growth.

→ Impact: Infrastructure bottlenecks could slow the rollout of always-on AI agents that platforms like Gmail and Outlook are betting on. If compute capacity gets constrained, the cost of running continuous inbox triage, meeting intelligence, and calendar agents goes up for everyone. This could actually benefit lighter, more efficient AI implementations — creating an opening for products that deliver intelligence without requiring massive always-on compute overhead.
