AI Isn't "Taking" Jobs — It's Dissolving and Rebuilding Every One

A CNN Business investigation finds that AI can technically automate 57% of work activities, according to McKinsey — but the percentage is spread across "pieces and parts" of every role. Companies aren't eliminating whole positions; they're dismantling jobs into tasks, automating what they can, and restructuring around what only humans can do.

The real-world evidence is stark. Cloudflare cut 1,100 employees (20% of workforce) after AI usage surged 600% in three months — despite posting record $639.8 million revenue. Coinbase slashed 14% of staff, with CEO Brian Armstrong noting engineers now ship in days what used to take weeks. Block eliminated nearly 40% of its workforce. AI was the top reason companies cited for job cuts in April, according to Challenger, Gray & Christmas.

Anthropic's head of Claude Code predicts the very term "software engineer" will evolve to "builder" by year's end as writing code becomes a smaller fraction of the role. The work isn't disappearing — it's being recomposed around a new human-AI division of labor.

→ Impact: As companies restructure around AI, the productivity tools that survive will be those that understand the new human-AI workflow — not just helping people do the old tasks faster, but reshaping how work itself is organized. Our product's AI features need to be designed for this recomposed workplace, where users are managing AI outputs as much as managing email.
