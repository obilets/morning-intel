Notion Launches Developer Platform — Becomes an AI Agent Hub

Notion shipped the biggest platform expansion in its history yesterday: a full Developer Platform that transforms the workspace app into an orchestration layer for AI agents. The headline features are Workers (a hosted runtime for custom code), an External Agents API, database sync from any source with an API, and a new CLI for developers and coding agents alike.

At launch, Claude Code, Cursor, Codex, and Decagon are supported as partner agents — meaning teams can chat with external AI directly inside Notion, assign work, and track progress as if the agent were a teammate. Workers are free through August while developers experiment.

CEO Ivan Zhao framed the ambition clearly: "Any data, any tool, any agent — that's the big picture." Notion is no longer just a note-taking app. It's positioning as the control room where people and agents collaborate across tools and databases.

→ Impact: Notion already has Mail and Calendar. Amazon is discontinuing its Rufus AI chatbot and merging it with Alexa+ into a new agentic shopping assistant called "Alexa for Shopping."
