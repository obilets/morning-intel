Google has declared that Gmail is officially entering the Gemini era, rolling out the most dramatic interface transformation in the service's twenty-two-year history. The centerpiece: AI Overviews — powered by Gemini 3 — that summarize long email threads on demand, surface key action items, and let users query their entire inbox in natural language. Ask Gmail "Who was the contractor that quoted the kitchen remodel last fall?" and it returns the answer instantly.

Beyond summaries, Google has upgraded Help Me Write with tone-matching that mimics individual writing styles, making AI-drafted replies nearly indistinguishable from human-composed ones. Simultaneously, Google is winding down Gmailify (third-party account linking) and POP protocol support, signaling a clear pivot toward a single-ecosystem, AI-first experience — and away from being a universal inbox.

The message is clear: both Google and Microsoft are optimizing for their own ecosystems, making cross-provider email management increasingly difficult through native tools.
