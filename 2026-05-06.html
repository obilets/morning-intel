GPT-5.5 Instant Is Now ChatGPT's Default — And It Reads Your Gmail

OpenAI rolled out GPT-5.5 Instant on May 5 as the new default model powering every ChatGPT conversation, retiring GPT-5.3 Instant after just three months. The upgrade is significant on its own merits — 52.5% fewer hallucinated claims on high-stakes medical, legal, and financial prompts, and responses that are 30% more concise — but it's the personalization layer that should have every email client paying attention.

For Plus and Pro subscribers, GPT-5.5 Instant now references past conversations, uploaded files, and connected Gmail accounts to deliver answers grounded in your personal context. A new "memory sources" feature shows users exactly where ChatGPT pulled its information, and lets them delete or correct anything it got wrong. The Gmail integration means ChatGPT can surface flight details, meeting confirmations, and project context without the user ever opening their inbox.

OpenAI plans to extend this personalization to Free, Go Business, and Enterprise users in the coming weeks, with mobile support following shortly after.

→ Impact: ChatGPT is steadily becoming the interface through which hundreds of millions of users interact with their email — not as a client, but as a conversational layer that makes dedicated inbox apps feel redundant. Each personalization upgrade tightens that grip. The team's AI features must deliver contextual intelligence that goes deeper than a general-purpose chatbot skimming Gmail metadata.

On the same stage, Dario Amodei delivered a sobering warning: Anthropic's restricted Mythos model has discovered almost 300 vulnerabilities in Firefox alone, and "thousands, probably by now tens of thousands" across other closed-door assessments. → Impact: The cybersecurity calculus has shifted dramatically. Any product that handles user email — our product included — is a high-value target. Security and encryption aren't differentiators anymore; they're table stakes that must evolve at AI speed.
