The round was led by Abstract and A* (Kevin Hartz), with participation from Felicis and Elad Gil.

Cloudflare Open-Sources "Agentic Inbox" — A Self-Hosted AI Email Client Anyone Can Deploy

During Cloudflare's Agents Week (April 9-16), developer Thomas Gauvin open-sourced Agentic Inbox — a complete, self-hosted email client with a built-in AI agent that runs entirely on Cloudflare Workers. The code is on GitHub and anyone can deploy it to their own Cloudflare account.

The AI agent reads your inbox, searches conversations, and drafts replies through a side panel with 9 email tools. It automatically processes inbound emails and generates draft replies, but always requires explicit user confirmation before sending. Each mailbox is isolated in its own Durable Object with a SQLite database, and attachments are stored in R2.

This is different from the Cloudflare Email Service beta (covered previously) — Agentic Inbox is a consumer-facing reference application that demonstrates what a complete AI email client looks like when built on modern agent infrastructure. It's a blueprint that any developer can fork and customize.

→ Impact Analysis

An open-source AI email client lowers the barrier to entry for new competitors dramatically. Any startup can fork Agentic Inbox and build a differentiated email product in weeks instead of months. The team should study this codebase — both as competitive intelligence and as a potential reference for how others are approaching AI email agent architecture.

Cora Exits Beta — The "Inbox as Feed" Experiment Goes Live

Cora, the AI email manager from the Every team, has exited beta and is now publicly available at $15/month. Its pitch: "Give Cora your inbox, take back your life." The product's key insight — turning email from an inbox into a feed — represents one of the more interesting UX experiments in the space.

Here's how it works: Cora auto-archives most of your email. Things only stay in your inbox if the AI thinks you need to reply. Twice a day, you get a "brief" — a scannable summary of everything that came in, organized by importance. Recent updates improved email thread visualization, redesigned brief notifications into useful snapshots, and refined the summarization system.

The approach is philosophically different from both traditional email clients (you manage everything) and agentic clients (AI manages everything). Cora sits in between: AI curates and summarizes, but you still decide what to act on.

→ Impact Analysis

The "inbox as feed" concept is worth studying as a design direction. At $15/month, Cora is a niche product, but its twice-daily briefing format could inform how our product approaches email summaries and priority views. The auto-archive-by-default approach is bold — monitor user reception to see if people are ready to let AI decide what stays in their inbox.

Microsoft Basic Auth Deadline Hits April 30 — OAuth-Only Era Begins in Two Days

Apr 30

The long-anticipated deadline arrives this Thursday: Microsoft will enforce 100% rejection of Basic Authentication for SMTP AUTH in Exchange Online. The enforcement began gradually on March 1, 2026 with a small percentage of rejections, ramping up throughout April. After April 30, any app using Basic Auth (username + password) for IMAP, POP, or SMTP connections to Microsoft 365 will simply stop working.

This isn't just a Microsoft story — it's the final nail in the coffin of password-based email access. Combined with Gmail's parallel Gmailify/POP shutdown, the two dominant email platforms now require OAuth 2.0 exclusively. Legacy desktop clients, older mobile apps, and automation scripts that haven't migrated will break simultaneously.

For users of smaller email clients that haven't implemented proper OAuth flows, Thursday will be the day their apps stop connecting to Microsoft 365 mailboxes entirely.

→ Impact Analysis

Beyond our product, this deadline will generate a wave of frustrated users whose current email apps break — creating a brief but real acquisition window for clients with robust OAuth support. Customer support should be prepared for an uptick in Microsoft 365 connection questions this week.
